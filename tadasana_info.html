TADASANA

Mountain Pose

Tadasana, or Mountain Pose, is a foundational standing yoga pose. It is often the first position used in Surya Namaskar (Sun Salutations) and serves as the starting and grounding posture for your entire yoga practice.

Tadasana in Sun Salutations

WHEN TO PERFORM TADASANA

While Tadasana is part of general yoga practice, it is noteworthy as the starting pose and final pose in most, if not all, Surya Namaskar sequences. As well as being used to start and finish sun salutations, Tadasana is one of the foundational Ashtanga yoga poses suitable for working on basic body awareness and control for other standing poses. Together with control and body awareness, it improves overall posture.

BENEFITS OF TADASANA

  • Balance of root and heart chakras
  • Improved concentration and balance
  • Body Scanning and enhanced proprioception
  • Better posture and alignment
  • Calmer mind and central nervous system regulation

HOW TO PERFORM TADASANA

  1. Stand with your feet slightly apart (hip-width is often preferred) and make sure that your weight is balanced equally on both feet.
  2. Inhale, raise your arms above your head, interlock your fingers with palms facing upwards.
  3. Raise your shoulders up towards your ears and on an exhale, roll your shoulders back and down your spine, opening your chest and straightening your posture.
  4. Relax all muscles in your face, including your tongue.
  5. Relax your eyes and maintain a steady gaze (Drishti).
  6. Come back to normal position and relax.

CONTRAINDICATIONS & CAUTIONS

  • Severe headache or migraine.
  • Low blood pressure (be cautious when raising the arms overhead).
  • Insomnia (consult a doctor).

WATCH OUT FOR

  • Foot grounding: Ensure even pressure across all four corners of both feet. Avoid locking your knees.
  • Rib flaring: Keep your lower ribs subtly tucked in to avoid arching your lower back excessively while reaching up.
