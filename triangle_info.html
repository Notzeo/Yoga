UTTHITA TRIKONASANA

Extended Triangle Pose

**Utthita Trikonasana**, or Extended Triangle Pose, is a foundational standing yoga pose. It is often included in yoga sequences as it helps to increase flexibility in the **hips, hamstrings, and spine**, while also strengthening the legs and core. This asymmetrical posture involves keeping both knees straight and bending the body sideways, which greatly improves overall spine mobility.

SANSKRIT MEANING

In Sanskrit, **Trikonasana** means Triangle pose or Three Angle pose. **Utthita** means extended, hence the full name, Extended Triangle Pose.

Extended Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

TRIKONASANA BENEFITS

  • Strengthens the legs and core.
  • Improves balance and coordination, challenging the practitioner's stability.
  • Stretches and strengthens the back muscles, alleviating tension and stiffness in the spine.
  • Stimulates the abdominal organs and aids digestion.
  • Calms the nervous system, making it an excellent exercise for relieving **stress** and anxiety.
  • Increases energy levels and stamina through physical effort.
  • Promotes good posture and alignment by strengthening the muscles that support the spine.

TRIKONASANA CONTRAINDICATIONS

  • **Low blood pressure:** Practice cautiously, as the posture can cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to dizziness.
  • **Neck or spine injuries:** Avoid or modify the pose to prevent putting pressure on the neck or spine.
  • **Pregnancy:** Modify the posture to avoid pressure on the abdomen; consult a qualified yoga teacher or doctor.
  • **High blood pressure:** Practice cautiously, as the pose can cause a sudden increase in blood pressure.

TRIKONASANA STEP-BY-STEP INSTRUCTIONS

No matter which variation you are practicing, the basic shape of the pose is constant. Here are the step-by-step instructions:

  1. Begin in **Tadasana** (Mountain Pose), standing straight with the feet hip-width apart and the arms at the sides.
  2. Step or jump the feet one leg's length apart. Turn the right foot out $90^\circ$ and the left foot slightly inwards.
  3. Inhale and lift the arms parallel to the floor at shoulder height, palms facing down.
  4. Exhale and extend your torso forward—bending from your **hip joint**, not the waist—to reach your side body directly over your front leg.
  5. Place the right hand on the right shin, ankle, or the floor outside the right foot. You can also use a block for support.
  6. Reach the left arm straight up toward the ceiling, aligning the arms with the shoulders. Gaze up at the left thumb or forward.
  7. Hold the pose for a few breaths, lengthening the spine on inhales and deepening the stretch on exhales.
  8. To release, inhale and lift the torso back up to a standing position, straighten the right leg, and return to Tadasana.
  9. Repeat the entire sequence on the other side.
