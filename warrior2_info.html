VIRABHADRASANA II

Warrior 2 Pose

Virabhadrasana II (Warrior 2 Pose), named after the Hindu god who incarnated as a human, enhances the strength and stamina of beginner and advanced yogis alike. In the pose, your front knee bends to create a stretch in your hips, your arms engage and extend straight out from your shoulders, and your gaze, or drishti, remains calm and steady toward your front hand. It is the second of three poses dedicated to Virabhadra.

Warrior II, focused and strong

THE BENEFITS OF VIRABHADRASANA II

The posture strengthens your core, including your abs and back muscles, as well as the hip flexors, gluteal muscles, inner thigh, and ankle of the bent leg. In your back leg, the pose stretches the hip flexors, hamstring, and calf muscles. Warrior II stretches your chest, stretches and strengthens your shoulders, and increases your endurance, one bent knee at a time.

CAUTIONS

Consult a healthcare professional if you have recent or chronic knee, hip, or shoulder injuries. Individuals with high blood pressure should avoid holding the arms overhead for long periods.

STEP-BY-STEP INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Start by facing the long side of your mat with your arms stretched straight out from your shoulders and your feet parallel to each other in a wide stance (ankles approximately beneath your wrists).
  2. Turn your right foot and knee to face the front of the mat. Angle your left toes slightly in toward the upper left corner of the mat.
  3. Bend your right knee and stack it directly over your right ankle. Ensure your thigh is parallel to the floor, forming a $90^\circ$ angle if possible.
  4. Distribute your weight evenly between both legs. Press down firmly through the outer edge of your back foot.
  5. Keep the crown of your head stacked over your pelvis and your shoulders aligned vertically over your hips.
  6. Reach strongly through both arms toward the front and back of the mat, keeping them parallel to the floor. Turn your head to look past your right fingertips.
  7. Stay here for $5$–$10$ deep breaths.
  8. To come out of the pose, exhale as you press down through your feet, then inhale and straighten your legs. Return your feet to parallel facing the long side of the mat.
  9. Repeat the sequence on the other side.

VIRABHADRASANA II VARIATIONS

Using a Wall for Stability

To help maintain your balance and ensure correct alignment in this pose, practice with your back to a wall. You can also place a block between your bent shin and the wall to help maintain the right angle and prevent your knee from tracking forward past your ankle.

Seated Chair Modification

For less strenuous practice, sit on the edge of a sturdy chair and move your thighs into the Warrior II position as outlined in the instructions above. Lift your arms parallel to the ground or keep your hands on your hips for support.
